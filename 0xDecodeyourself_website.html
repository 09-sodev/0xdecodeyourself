Learn From Sebastian

Start here. Right now.

Tip 01
Your LinkedIn headline is your billboard.
Stop writing "Student at XYZ University." Write who you are and what you bring. Example: "Marketing strategist and aspiring brand leader | Passionate about consumer psychology." Own your identity before you have the title.
Tip 02
Consistency beats perfection.
Your personal brand is built post by post, conversation by conversation. You don't need to have it all figured out. You need to show up consistently in the spaces where your future employers are watching.
Tip 03
Your story is your strategy.
The most powerful brand asset you have is your "why." Why this field? Why now? Why you? Get that story clear and you'll stand out in every room you walk into — even before you say a word about your GPA.
"Your personal brand is not what you post. It's what people say about you when you leave the room."
— Sebastian Oddo

Tip 01
Recruiters spend 6–7 seconds on your resume.
Your resume needs to pass the skim test. Use bold headers, clean spacing, and lead every bullet with a strong action verb. If someone can't identify your #1 achievement in 5 seconds, rewrite it.
Tip 02
Quantify everything you possibly can.
"Led marketing initiatives" is forgettable. "Grew campus club social media by 340% in one semester, reaching 2,400 students" is not. Numbers make your experience real and verifiable — use them everywhere.
Tip 03
Tailor for ATS — don't write for robots, but don't ignore them.
Applicant Tracking Systems scan for keywords. Mirror the language in the job description. If they say "cross-functional collaboration," use those exact words. You're not gaming the system — you're speaking its language.
"A great resume doesn't get you the job. It gets you the conversation. Make sure it earns that chance."
— Sebastian Oddo

Tip 01
Start with "What energizes me?" not "What pays well?"
Salary matters, but it's not a career strategy. Start by mapping the intersection of what you're good at, what energizes you, and what the market actually needs. That's where lasting careers are built.
Tip 02
Internships are experiments, not commitments.
Use every internship as a data point. What did you love? What drained you? What kind of team culture made you do your best work? You're gathering intel, not signing a life contract. Stay curious.
Tip 03
Build a 3-year vision, not a 10-year plan.
The job market changes too fast for 10-year plans. But having a 3-year north star — a role, a skill set, an industry — gives you direction without locking you in. Revisit and adjust every year.
"Clarity isn't something you find. It's something you build — through action, reflection, and the courage to adjust."
— Sebastian Oddo

Tip 01
Networking is not asking for favors. It's building relationships.
The fastest way to ruin a networking opportunity is to open with "Can you get me a job?" Lead with curiosity. Ask about their path, their lessons, their advice. People love talking about themselves — let them.
Tip 02
The informational interview is your most underused tool.
A 20-minute "coffee chat" with someone at your target company is worth more than 50 cold applications. It gives you insider intel, a warm connection, and often an internal referral. Start asking. Most people say yes.
Tip 03
Follow up — always.
80% of networking value is lost in the follow-up gap. Send a LinkedIn note within 24 hours of meeting someone. Reference something specific from your conversation. One thoughtful message turns a contact into a connection.
"Your network is not a transaction. It's a garden. You have to tend it long before you need it."
— Sebastian Oddo
